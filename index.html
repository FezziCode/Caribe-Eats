Arroz con Gandules

Traditional Puerto Rican rice with pigeon peas dish.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups white rice
  • 1 can (15 oz) pigeon peas
  • 4 cups water
  • 1/2 cup sofrito sauce
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon salt

Instructions:

  1. Rinse rice under cold water until water runs clear.
  2. In a large pot, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add sofrito and sauté for 2-3 minutes.
  3. Add rice, water, pigeon peas, and salt. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 20-25 minutes, or until rice is cooked and liquid is absorbed.
  4. Fluff rice with a fork before serving.

Mofongo

A classic Puerto Rican dish made with mashed plantains.

Ingredients:

  • 2 green plantains
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/4 cup olive oil
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Peel and slice plantains into chunks.
  2. Heat olive oil in a skillet over medium heat. Fry plantains until golden brown.
  3. Remove plantains from skillet and mash with garlic and salt.
  4. Form mashed plantains into balls and serve hot.

Tostones

Fried green plantains, a popular Puerto Rican appetizer.

Ingredients:

  • 2 green plantains
  • Oil for frying
  • Salt to taste

Instructions:

  1. Peel plantains and cut them into 1-inch slices.
  2. Heat oil in a skillet over medium-high heat.
  3. Fry plantain slices until golden brown, then remove and drain excess oil.
  4. Using a tostonera or flat surface, flatten the fried plantain slices.
  5. Return flattened plantains to the skillet and fry again until crispy.
  6. Sprinkle with salt and serve hot.

Pastelón

Puerto Rican plantain "lasagna" with ground beef and cheese.

Ingredients:

  • 4 ripe plantains
  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 bell pepper, diced
  • 1 cup tomato sauce
  • 2 cups shredded cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions:

  1. Peel and slice plantains lengthwise, then boil until tender.
  2. In a skillet, brown ground beef with onion, garlic, and bell pepper. Season with salt and pepper.
  3. Layer half of the boiled plantains in a baking dish, followed by half of the ground beef mixture and half of the cheese. Repeat layers.
  4. Bake at 350°F (175°C) for 20-25 minutes, or until cheese is melted and bubbly.
  5. Serve hot and enjoy!
